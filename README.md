# Retail_Data_Analysis
 ## PROJECT OBJECTIVE:
Analyzed relational database consisting of 9 tables using SQL server (SSMS) to transform raw data into actionable business insights including product performance, store performance, and customer geographic demographics.

## DATASET:
The dataset contains following tables: orders, orderItems, brands, categories, products, customers, stores, staffs, and stocks.

## SQL Concepts Demonstrated:

**Data Filtering & Pattern Matching:** Utilizing *WHERE*, *BETWEEN*, and *LIKE* for precise data extraction.

**Aggregations & Analytics:** Using *COUNT*, *SUM*, *AVG*, *ROUND*, *MIN*, and *MAX* to compute business metrics.

**Group-Level Analysis:** Leveraging *GROUP BY* and *HAVING* to isolate high-performing product segments and brands.

**Sorting:** Using *ORDER BY* and *DISTINCT* for clean reporting.

## KEY BUISNESS INSIGHTS & FINDINGS:

**1. Sales Performance**

* Total Revenue Generated: $8.5M

* Total Order Volume: 1,615 unique transactions

* Average Order Value (AOV): $5,312.07

**2. Store Performance**

* Store 2 dominated operations, handling the highest volume with 1,093 orders. Store 1 followed with 348 orders, while Store 3 trailed last with 174 orders.

* When it comes to staff productivity, staff members 6 (553 orders) and 7 (540 orders) were the top organizational performers. However, staff member 9 was the least active, processing only 86 orders.

**3. Customer Demographics**

Grouped customer volume by state. This may help business in marketing campaign

**4. Inventory Health**

* Identified the Top 5 Best-Performing Products

* Pinpointed 14 products that have never been sold. This helps business in reducing cost and clearance strategy planning.
